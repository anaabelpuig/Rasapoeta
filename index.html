Gracias por permitir / Thank You for Allowing

Gracias por permitir

Iniciar este caminar

Lanzar a las ondas es mi opción,

Conseguir resultados, tu logro.

Lanza a las ondas, me dices,

Que yo te daré la respuesta.

Tan alta y clara es tu llegada,

Como grisácea mi meta.

Encontrarás las herramientas,

Y con tu canto entonarás,

Pasajes constantes de verdad,

Salmos a contraluz

que iluminen tu pensar,

Y, al llegar, mientras caminas,

Despejada esa penumbra tendrás.

Thank You for Allowing

To begin this walk--thank you.

To cast into the waves is my choice,

To bring results--your grace.

"Cast into the waves," you tell me,

"And I shall give you the answer."

So clear and high your coming rings,

As gray and veiled my own goal clings.

You'll find the tools you need,

And with your voice you'll sing

Steady passages of truth,

Psalms in backlight

That will brighten your thoughts.

And as you walk, upon your way,

That twilight veil will fade away.

Nota curatorial / Curatorial Note: Este poema se dirige a una presencia íntima, a una parte de uno mismo, con ternura y distancia. El yo poético se da parcialmente, como luz dosificada. Se habla de energía contagiosa, de entusiasmo que no se entrega del todo, sino que espera el momento justo para esparcirse. Los girasoles, símbolo de esa contención luminosa, representan el deseo de guardar el sol: de convertir lo inabarcable en algo que pueda mirarse sin herir. El poema gira en torno a esa paradoja: amar sin invadir, brillar sin desbordar.

This poem speaks to an intimate presence—perhaps to a part of oneself—with tenderness and distance. The poetic voice gives only part of itself, as measured light. It speaks of contagious energy, of an enthusiasm not yet released, waiting for the right moment to scatter. The sunflowers, symbol of that luminous containment, represent the desire to hold the sun: to turn the ungraspable into something one can gaze upon without harm. The poem turns around that paradox: to love without invading, to shine without overflowing.
